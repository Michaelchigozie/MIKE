LAND USE AND LAND COVER ANALYSIS

Overview

This project provides a detailed analysis of the Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) changes that have occurred in Enugu State Metropolis over a 33-year period (1990–2023). The study captures the dynamic transformations in land use patterns caused by urbanization, population growth, infrastructural development, and environmental factors. By utilizing advanced geospatial techniques and satellite imagery, the research offers a comprehensive understanding of how urban expansion has reshaped the landscape, affecting vegetation, water bodies, and open spaces. The project focuses on quantifying changes in key LULC categories, such as built-up areas, dense vegetation, bare land, and water, providing critical data for sustainable development. Through this analysis, the study identifies trends such as the significant increase in urban areas and the decline of vegetation cover, emphasizing the need for effective land management strategies. The insights gained from this research are intended to aid urban planners, policymakers, and environmentalists in making informed decisions about land use, conservation, and urban development.
Furthermore, the project aims to highlight the environmental implications of these changes, including increased flood risks, loss of biodiversity, and potential impacts on climate regulation. The findings serve as a valuable resource for addressing challenges related to rapid urbanization and environmental degradation, while also fostering discussions around sustainable development practices.
By integrating LULC maps, statistical analyses, and visualizations, the project provides an engaging and accessible platform for understanding these changes. This research ultimately seeks to contribute to a balanced approach that promotes economic growth while ensuring environmental sustainability and improved quality of life for the residents of Enugu State Metropolis.

Objective

The primary goal of this project is to:

1.


Assess the spatial and temporal changes in land use and land cover from 1990 to 2023.

2.


Quantify the shifts in key LULC categories such as built-up areas, vegetation, bare land, and water bodies.

3.


Provide data-driven recommendations to guide sustainable urban development and environmental conservation in Enugu Metropolis.

Methodology

Data Collection

Utilized Landsat satellite imagery (1990, 1995, 2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, 2020, and 2023) for the LULC analysis.
Pre-processed data to correct for atmospheric distortions and geometric errors.

Classification Technique:

Applied Supervised Classification with the Maximum Likelihood Classifier (MLC) in ArcGIS. Used training samples representing distinct land cover classes (Dense Vegetation, Vegetation, Bare Land, Built-up Area, Water).

Validation:

 Conducted accuracy assessments using a confusion matrix and kappa coefficient for each year’s classification to ensure reliability.

Change Detection:

 Performed post-classification comparison to calculate the net and proportional changes in each LULC class over the study period.

Findings


LULC Dynamics:


Built-up Areas:

Increased from 9% in 1990 to 31% in 2023, reflecting rapid urbanization.

Dense Vegetation:

Declined significantly from 45% in 1990 to 15% in 2023, indicating deforestation and land degradation.

Vegetation:

Reduced from 31% to 19% over the same period.

Bare Land:

Increased moderately from 13% to 32%, highlighting urban sprawl and construction activities.

Water Bodies:

Slight variations were observed, remaining consistent at around 1%.

Graphical Insights:

Pie charts and bar graphs depict the proportional change in each LULC class over time. The area change chart visualizes the extent of changes in hectares for all LULC categories.

Key Observations:


Urbanization is the leading driver of LULC changes.
Loss of vegetation and open spaces poses environmental challenges such as increased flooding risk and biodiversity loss.

Challenges and Solutions

Challenge: Handling Landsat 7 data gaps due to scan line errors.
Solution: Used the Landsat 7 Toolbox to correct and fill data gaps.
Challenge: Classification inaccuracies due to spectral similarities between bare land and built-up areas. Solution: Applied advanced pre-processing techniques and field verification. BOOM!, THE ERROR IS CORRECTED

Recommendations

 1. Implement green urban planning practices to mitigate environmental impacts.

2. Promote afforestation and reforestation programs to restore vegetation cover.

3. Strengthen land-use policies to regulate unplanned urban expansion.

4. Conduct periodic LULC assessments to monitor environmental changes and inform decision-making.

Tools and Technologies



1. ArcGIS: LULC classification and change detection.

2. Landsat Data: Satellite imagery acquisition.

3. Excel: Data tabulation and visualization.

4. Golden Software Surfer: Enhanced 3D visualization for comparative analysis.

Conclusion



This comprehensive LULC analysis provides valuable insights into the urbanization and environmental transformation of Enugu State Metropolis over three decades.
The findings underscore the urgent need for sustainable urban management practices to balance development and environmental conservation.
