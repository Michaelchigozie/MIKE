Total PM in Texas 2023:

Color Range & Legend:
The color scale ranges from light green to dark red, representing increasing concentrations of particulate matter (PM2.5 or PM10). The concentration values in the legend indicate:

  • Green (lower PM): 4.40 to 6.49
  • Yellow (medium PM): 7.81 to 9.86
  • Orange/Red (higher PM): 9.86 to 13.75

Spatial Distribution:
The central and western parts of Texas show lower levels of particulate matter (green to yellow zones), indicating relatively cleaner air in these regions. In contrast, the eastern and southern parts of Texas (orange to red zones) show higher PM concentrations, suggesting regions with poorer air quality. The clustering of dark red areas highlights regions experiencing the highest pollution levels, likely urbanized or industrial areas.

AQI of Texas 2023:

Color Range & Legend:
The AQI map also uses a color gradient from green (good) to red (unhealthy), corresponding to AQI values:

  • Green: 32.19 to 32.56 (good air quality)
  • Yellow: 32.56 to 39.43 (moderate air quality)

Relationship Between PM and AQI:

The graph on the right compares PM and AQI for Texas in 2023. There appears to be a positive correlation between PM and AQI; as PM increases, AQI also rises, reflecting worsening air quality. This suggests that particulate matter levels significantly influence the air quality in Texas.
